Programming and Development

My usual setup is a zsh terminal on an Ubuntu machine and my project opened in my favorite IDE PyCharm. I use Python on a daily basis and most of my projects are written in Python. Mostly, I use it in a combination with TensorFlow or PyTorch since a vast majority of my projects involve machine learning. However, I am also familiar with C++ from my student jobs. For example, I implemented a lane detection on LiDAR point clouds with Caffe. The first programming language that I learned was Java. So far, I only implemented one project, a tile-based board game called Carcassonne. During my studies at University, I used Matlab a lot, for example for my Bachelor Thesis project.

Competitions

In the last two years I participated in some coding competitions.

  • Google Code Jam: It's my first year, so let's see how far I get...

  • Google Hash Code:
    • 2020: We had a new team strategy and made 824th place out of 10724 teams. Find our code on GitHub.
    • 2019: This was our first try as a team. Unfortunately, we only reached place 5075 out of 6640 teams.
  • IEEE SB Passau Advent Calendar:
    A variety of simple to difficult coding puzzles in christmas themes with up to 50 participants. I participated in 2018 and 2019 and made it to the top 15 and 10, respectively.

  • ZEISS and KIT Robotics Innovation Competition (2018):
    A 24-hour hackathon in teams of four where we had to solve the cup game with a camera and a robot arm. We implemented a detection and tracking algorithm for our three cups and the ball. After shuffling the cups, we control the robot arm via ROS to lift the cup with the ball underneath.
